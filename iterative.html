What is Iteration in Terms of Design Thinking?

Design Thinking thrives on iteration, embodying a continual feedback loop that prompts designers to revisit and refine their approaches based on user input. This dynamic cycle, embedded in the process's core, ensures that the solutions evolve iteratively, progressively aligning with user needs. Designers actively seek feedback at each stage, fostering adaptability and a commitment to improvement. The iterative nature acknowledges that initial assumptions may be incomplete, encouraging a continuous learning mindset. This approach enables designers to refine problem definitions, ideation outcomes, and prototypes, ultimately shaping solutions that authentically resonate with users' experiences and preferences. Iteration, therefore, isn't merely a step in the process but a strategic commitment to crafting more effective, user-centric, and innovative outcomes.
